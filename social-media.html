Angles

1. Eye Level Shot

Camera placed at the subject’s eye level for a natural and balanced perspective.

2. Low Angle Shot

Camera positioned below the subject, looking upward to create power and dominance.

3. High Angle Shot

Camera placed above the subject, looking downward for a softer or more vulnerable feel.

4. Top Down Shot

Direct overhead perspective capturing the subject from above in a graphic composition.

5. Dutch Angle

Tilted camera angle creating tension, energy, or cinematic unease.

6. Close Up

Framing tightly around the face to emphasize emotion and detail.

7. Extreme Close Up

Ultra-tight framing focused on a facial detail like eyes or lips.

8. Macro Shot

Extreme detail shot revealing texture, skin, reflections, or wine details.

9. Over the Shoulder Shot

Camera positioned behind one subject looking toward another for conversational intimacy.

10. Point of View Shot

Perspective simulating exactly what the character sees.

11. Hero Shot

Strong cinematic framing making the subject appear iconic and aspirational.

12. Packshot Front View

Clean centered product shot with frontal symmetry and minimal distraction.

13. Three Quarter View

Subject turned slightly away from camera for dimensional depth.

14. Side Profile Shot

Pure side angle emphasizing silhouette and facial structure.

15. Back Label Shot

Focus on the rear wine label for authenticity and product detail.

16. Bottle Neck Detail

Tight crop highlighting capsule, foil, embossing, or bottle neck texture.

17. Pouring Shot

Captures wine being poured with dynamic liquid movement.

18. Glass Reflection Shot

Uses reflections through the wine glass for layered cinematic composition.

19. Table Level Shot

Low camera placement aligned with the table surface for immersive realism.

20. Foreground Blur Shot

Foreground objects intentionally blurred to create depth and focus.

21. Background Reveal Shot

Composition revealing landscape or environment behind the subject.

22. Wide Establishing Shot

Wide cinematic frame introducing the full environment and atmosphere.

23. Medium Lifestyle Shot

Balanced lifestyle framing showing both subject and surroundings naturally.

24. Tracking Shot

Motion shot following the subject to create movement and cinematic flow.

25. Melancholic Reflection

Thoughtful emotional mood with quiet introspection.

26. Magnetic Presence

Strong visual charisma naturally attracting viewer attention.

27. Untamed Freedom

Wind, movement, and spontaneity expressing liberation and wildness.

28. Quiet Seduction

Subtle sensuality expressed through restrained body language and gaze.

29. Inner Strength

Emotionally grounded presence expressing resilience and calm power.

30. Nostalgic Warmth

Golden light and soft atmosphere evoking memory and comfort.

31. Lost in Thought

Subject appearing mentally distant and absorbed in reflection.

32. Electric Tension

Charged emotional atmosphere with intense cinematic energy.
