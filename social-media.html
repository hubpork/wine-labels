Angles, Facial Expressions und Camera Movements sind die drei wichtigsten Ebenen für cineastisches Visual Storytelling. Sie bestimmen nicht nur wie etwas aussieht, sondern wie es sich emotional anfühlt.

Angles

Camera Angles definieren die Perspektive der Kamera auf das Motiv. Sie beeinflussen Macht, Nähe, Dominanz, Verletzlichkeit oder Spannung.

  • Low Angle Shot → Macht, Autorität, Heroismus
  • High Angle Shot → Verletzlichkeit, Ruhe, Unterordnung
  • Eye Level Shot → Natürlichkeit, Ehrlichkeit, Balance
  • Dutch Angle → Spannung, Chaos, Unruhe
  • Top Down Shot → grafische Kontrolle, Isolation, Ästhetik

Angles verändern also die psychologische Wahrnehmung der Figur.

1. Eye Level Shot

Camera placed at the subject’s eye level for a natural and balanced perspective.

2. Low Angle Shot

Camera positioned below the subject, looking upward to create power and dominance.

3. High Angle Shot

Camera placed above the subject, looking downward for a softer or more vulnerable feel.

4. Top Down Shot

Direct overhead perspective capturing the subject from above in a graphic composition.

5. Dutch Angle

Tilted camera angle creating tension, energy, or cinematic unease.

6. Close Up

Ultra-tight framing focused on a facial detail like eyes or lips.

8. Macro Shot

Camera Movements

via aishotstudio.com - 42-camera-movements-ai-prompts

Camera Movements erzeugen Dynamik, Rhythmus und Immersion. Sie lassen ein Bild lebendig und filmisch wirken.

  • Tracking Shot → Bewegung, Flow, Begleitung
  • Push In → emotionale Intensität
  • Pull Back → Distanz oder Offenbarung
  • Handheld Movement → Authentizität, Rohheit
  • Slow Orbit Shot → Eleganz, cineastische Präsenz

Movements beeinflussen wie sich eine Szene „anfühlt“ in Zeit und Raum.

1. Slow Dolly In

Camera slowly moves forward toward the subject, creating intimacy, tension, or emotional focus.

2. Slow Dolly Out

Camera slowly pulls backward away from the subject, creating isolation or emotional distance.

3. Fast Dolly In

Rapid forward camera movement adding urgency, shock, or dramatic intensity.

4. Vertigo Effect (Zolly)

Camera moves backward while zooming inward, creating spatial distortion and psychological tension.

5. Extreme Macro Zoom

Extreme zoom into tiny details like skin, eyes, texture, or surfaces for hyper realism.

6. Cosmic Hyper Zoom

Massive cinematic zoom transition from wide environment into microscopic detail.

7. Over the Shoulder (OTS) Shot

Camera positioned behind one subject looking toward another to create conversational immersion.

8. Fisheye or Peephole Lens

Ultra wide distorted lens creating exaggerated perspective and surreal framing.

9. Reveal from Behind

Wipe movement where the camera slides laterally from behind a foreground object to reveal the scene.

10. Through Shot

Camera moves through an opening, doorway, frame, or object into the scene.

11. Reveal from Blur

Shot begins out of focus and slowly sharpens to reveal the subject clearly.

12. Rack Focus

Focus shifts from a foreground object to the background subject, or the other way around.

13. Tilt Up

Camera tilts vertically upward from bottom to top to reveal the subject or environment.

14. Tilt Down

Camera tilts vertically downward from top to bottom for a controlled reveal.

15. Camera Truck Left

Camera moves sideways on a track or stable path to the left.

16. Lateral Truck Right

Camera moves sideways on a track or stable path to the right.

17. Orbit 180 (Spin)

Camera or subject rotates halfway around to create movement, reveal, and cinematic energy.

18. Fast 360 Orbit (Spin)

Fast circular movement around the subject creating dynamic momentum and dramatic impact.

19. True Orbit 180

Camera moves in a controlled half-circle around the subject while keeping focus locked.

20. True Orbit 360

Camera fully circles around the subject for an immersive and cinematic perspective shift.

21. Slow Cinematic Arc

Camera slowly arcs around the subject to create elegance, depth, and emotional tension.

22. Pedestal Down

Camera moves vertically downward while keeping the same angle and framing control.

23. Pedestal Up

Camera moves vertically upward to reveal posture, presence, or heroic perspective.

24. Crane Up (High Angle Reveal)

Camera rises upward into a high angle to reveal scale, space, and environment.

25. Crane Down (Landing)

Camera descends slowly toward the subject, creating a controlled landing reveal.

26. Smooth Optical Zoom In

Lens smoothly magnifies the subject while the camera remains stationary.

27. Smooth Optical Zoom Out

Lens smoothly widens away from the subject, revealing more of the surrounding scene.

28. Snap Zoom (Crash Zoom)

A rapid zoom directly into the subject, creating surprise, comedy, or dramatic impact.

29. Drone Fly Over

High-altitude drone movement flying forward over the landscape.

30. Epic Drone Reveal

Drone rises or tilts to reveal a large environment with cinematic scale.

31. Large Scale Drone Orbit

Drone circles widely around a landscape, building grandeur and spatial drama.

32. Top Down (God's Eye View)

Camera points straight downward from above, creating a graphic overhead perspective.

33. FPV Drone Aggressive (Drone Dive)

High-speed FPV drone dive creating intense motion and adrenaline-filled perspective.

34. Handheld Documentary Style

Natural handheld camera movement with subtle shake for raw documentary realism.

35. Whip Pan

Fast horizontal camera whip creating motion blur and energetic scene transitions.

36. Dutch Angle (Roll)

Tilted camera roll creating tension, instability, or psychological unease.

37. Leading Shot (Backward Tracking)

Camera moves backward in front of the subject while matching their movement speed.

38. Following Shot (Forward Tracking)

Camera follows directly behind the subject to create immersion and cinematic flow.

39. Side Tracking (Parallel)

Camera tracks sideways alongside the moving subject for smooth cinematic motion.

40. POV Walk (First Person Walk)

First-person perspective movement simulating realistic walking motion and immersion.

41. Hyperlapse (Moving Time-Lapse)

Fast moving time-lapse sequence combining accelerated motion with dynamic camera travel.

42. Barrel Roll (Vortex Inception Shot)

Camera spins in a full 360-degree roll while moving forward for a disorienting cinematic effect.

43. Bullet Time (Frozen Moment)

Ultra slow-motion effect where time appears frozen while the camera moves around the subject.

44. Worm’s Eye Tracking (Ground Level)

Extreme low-angle tracking shot moving along the ground while looking upward.
